Angular Routes Lesson 1

Route Basics

Angular Router is the map between browser URLs and components. A route is like a labeled doorway: when the URL matches the label, Angular loads the component behind that doorway and places it in the app's router outlet.

What To Remember

  1. app.routes.ts is the top-level route table.
  2. loadChildren is useful for a category with many child routes.
  3. loadComponent lazy-loads one standalone component.
  4. routerLink asks Angular Router to navigate without refreshing the browser.
  5. router-outlet is where the matched component appears.
